# Vendor Onboarding Policy (v2.4)

**Owner:** Procurement & Vendor Management
**Applies to:** Onboarding of all third-party vendors and suppliers.

## Section 1. Purpose
Defines the mandatory steps to onboard a new vendor before any contract, payment, or system access is granted.

## Section 2. Vendor Profile
- **Section 2.1** — Every new vendor requires a completed **vendor profile form** (legal name, business owner, contact email, tax ID, category).
- **Section 2.2** — A vendor cannot proceed past onboarding while mandatory profile fields are missing.

## Section 3. NDA Requirement
- **Section 3.1** — Every vendor that will receive **confidential information** requires a signed **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)** before any system access.
- **Section 3.2** — The NDA must be drafted and routed to Legal for review. The agent may draft and route it, but only Legal may mark it executed.

## Section 4. Security Review
- **Section 4.1** — Every vendor requesting access to **internal systems** requires a **Security review** before access is provisioned.
- **Section 4.2** — Security review cannot be skipped, even for urgent requests.

## Section 5. Risk Tiering
- **Section 5.1** — Vendors handling customer data or with contract value above ₹5,00,000 are classified **high risk** and require Legal + Security + Finance sign-off.
- **Section 5.2** — Low-risk vendors (no data access, under ₹2,00,000) may be fast-tracked with Department Head approval.

## Section 6. Sequence
Vendor profile → NDA (if confidential) → Security review → Procurement approval → Access provisioning. Access is never provisioned before all required approvals are complete.
